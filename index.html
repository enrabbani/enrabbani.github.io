Projects

VEX Robotics Project – High Stakes Season (2024–2025)

What?

  • The main objective of the Vex High Stakes game was to take circular rings and put them on mobile ring stands.
  • We divided this task into three different systems: the intake (picking up rings from the field), the conveyor belt (scoring those rings onto the mobile ring stands), and the clamp (holding onto the mobile ring stand to allow the robot to score).

How?

  • Came up with basic designs in Microsoft OneNote through discussions over Discord.
  • Our first robot was a prototype and was able to perform the basic functions of the game but was structurally weak and frequently curved to the left instead of driving straight.
  • By contrast, our second robot had improved structural quality and performed the main functions of the game consistently and reliability. However, towards the end we had similar problems with the robot curving to one side instead of driving straight.

Results

  • Positives: Second robot was structurally sound and performed the main objectives of the game consistently and reliably.
  • Negatives: Our final robot had a faulty drivebase and lacked speed. Additionally, our robot couldn't perform the advanced tasks of the game such as climbing or putting rings on wall stakes.
  • Overall, both Max and I learned a lot from this experience. We learned critical lessons about drivetrain geometry, frame rigidity, and the importance of precise mechanical planning.
